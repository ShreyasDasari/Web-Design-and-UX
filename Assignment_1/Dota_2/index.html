Defence of the Ancients 2

Dota 2 is a popular multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game developed and published by Valve Corporation.

The game is a sequel to Defense of the Ancients (DotA), which was a community-created mod for Blizzard Entertainment's Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos.

In Dota 2, two teams of five players each compete to destroy the opposing team's Ancient, a large structure located within the enemy base.

Each player controls a unique hero with their own abilities and characteristics.

The game features a wide variety of heroes to choose from, each with their own strengths and weaknesses.

Some heroes excel at dealing damage, while others are better at supporting and protecting their allies.

Players can earn gold and experience by killing enemy heroes, minions, and buildings, and can use this gold to buy items that will help them in battle.

The game also features a complex item system, where players can combine basic items to create more powerful ones.

The game features a variety of game modes, including the standard 5v5 match, a 1v1 mode, and a "captains mode" where players take turns selecting heroes for their team.
